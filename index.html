CURATORIAL CONCEPT

The Narrative Thread

The exhibition is guided by the central theme: "Pagkakakilanlan" — Filipino Identity Through Art. It explores how identity has been shaped by colonial history, global influences, personal narratives, and social realities across different time periods.

"Pagkakakilanlan"

Filipino identity is shaped not by a single narrative but by layered histories — colonial struggle, cultural exchange, personal memory, and everyday life. This exhibition brings these layers together to show that identity is both inherited and continuously redefined.

Colonial Experience & Struggle

Spoliarium and The Assassination of Governor Bustamante reflect the violence, oppression, and political tensions of colonial rule. These works reveal how Filipino artists used historical narratives to critique injustice and express a growing sense of national consciousness.

Global Influence & Identity

The Parisian Life demonstrates how Filipino artists engaged with European modernity. While rooted in foreign settings, the work subtly reflects the presence and identity of Filipinos abroad, negotiating between local identity and global culture.

Personal Identity & Emotion

Portrait of Laureana Novicio y Ancheta presents a more intimate dimension of identity. Through portraiture, the artwork highlights family, memory, and emotional depth, emphasizing the personal side of Filipino identity.

Social Reality & Everyday Life

Hulat Sweldo and Jeepneys shift the focus to modern Filipino life. These works depict labor, urbanization, and daily struggle, showing how identity is continuously shaped by social conditions and collective experience.

A Guardian of Filipino Heritage

The National Museum of the Philippines is the country’s leading institution dedicated to the preservation, study, and promotion of Filipino cultural and natural heritage. Located along Padre Burgos Avenue in Manila, the museum complex is situated within the historic Rizal Park area and is composed of several significant heritage buildings.

Established in 1901 as the Insular Museum, the institution has evolved into a network of specialized museums covering fine arts, anthropology, natural history, and geology. Today, it serves as a vital center for education, research, and cultural preservation, housing hundreds of thousands of artifacts and artworks that reflect the richness and diversity of Philippine history.

Address Padre Burgos Ave, Ermita, Manila, 1000 Metro Manila

Rich History

Established in 1901, the National Museum has served as the primary institution for preserving Philippine heritage for over a century.

National Significance

The museum houses numerous National Cultural Treasures and important artifacts that represent the country's artistic, historical, and scientific legacy.

Art Collections

It is home to iconic masterpieces such as Juan Luna’s Spoliarium and works by renowned Filipino artists, making it the central repository of Philippine fine arts.

Cultural Mission

The museum is committed to preserving, protecting, and promoting Philippine heritage for present and future generations through education and public engagement.

GROUP REFLECTION

Insights & Takeaways

A collective reflection on what this journey taught us about art, culture, identity, and the power of collaboration.

What We Learned

This project deepened our understanding of Philippine art history far beyond what textbooks can offer. We came to realize that each artwork is not merely a visual creation, but a reflection of its time — shaped by social conditions, political realities, and cultural identity. Through our analysis, we learned to look beyond the surface and ask deeper questions: why was this created, what message does it convey, and how does it relate to Filipino identity today? This process helped us develop critical thinking and a greater appreciation for the meaning behind art.

Importance of Preservation

Throughout this project, we recognized that Philippine art is a vital part of our national identity. It connects us to our history, our struggles, and our collective experiences as Filipinos. In an era of rapid modernization and digital transformation, preserving these works is more important than ever. We realized that preservation is not only the responsibility of institutions, but also of individuals. By creating this digital museum, we contributed in our own way to keeping Filipino culture alive and accessible to a wider audience.

Our Experience

Creating this digital exhibit was both a challenging and rewarding experience. As a team, we encountered difficulties such as managing time, dividing responsibilities, and ensuring consistency in both design and content. However, these challenges taught us the value of communication, collaboration, and trust. Each member played a crucial role, and by combining our individual strengths, we were able to produce a cohesive and meaningful output. This experience showed us that successful projects are not only built on skills, but also on teamwork and shared commitment.
